Pharmaceutical giant Pfizer announced a $68 billion acquisition of biotech startup Seagen on March 10, 2026. Pfizer CEO Albert Bourla described the deal as "transformative for our oncology pipeline." Seagen CEO David Epstein called it "the right outcome for patients and shareholders."

The deal requires approval from the Federal Trade Commission and the European Commission's Directorate-General for Competition. Antitrust lawyer Tim Wu, now at Columbia Law School, warned the deal could face scrutiny under new merger guidelines.

Wall Street reacted positively: Morgan Stanley upgraded Pfizer to "overweight" and Barclays raised its price target. However, activist investor Carl Icahn purchased a 3% stake in Seagen, demanding a higher premium.

The Pharmaceutical Research and Manufacturers of America (PhRMA) praised the consolidation trend. Meanwhile, Doctors Without Borders criticized the deal, arguing it would reduce competition in cancer drug pricing. The World Health Organization expressed concerns about market concentration.

Competitor Roche reportedly considered a counter-bid but ultimately declined. Bristol-Myers Squibb was rumored to be evaluating its own acquisition targets in response.
